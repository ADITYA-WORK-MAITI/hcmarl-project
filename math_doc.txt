1 Executive Summary

This document presents the complete, line-by-line veried mathematical architecture for the
Human-Centric Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning (HC-MARL) framework. The following
corrections have been implemented relative to previous iterations:

C1. MMICRL objective: The weighted entropy objective 1H[( )] - 2H[( | z)] is

not equivalent to mutual information I ( ; z) unless 1 = 2. The objective is now correctly

characterised as a weighted information-theoretic trade-o with an explicit residual term.

C2. ECBF switched-mode proof: The awed rst-derivative argument is replaced by a

direct Nagumo invariance proof on h(x) = max - MF  0, yielding an explicit and veriable
design requirement max  F /(F + Rr) for each muscle group.

C3. Dual safety barrier: A second barrier function h2(x) = MR  0 is introduced to enforce

physiological validity of the resting compartment, preventing transient recruitment from an
empty pool.

C4. Nash Social Welfare: The centralised allocation objective max{aij} i ln(U (i, j(i))-Di)

is correctly identied as maximising the Nash Social Welfare Function (NSWF), not Nash Bar-
gaining in the game-theoretic sense. The axiomatic connection to Nash's framework is preserved
through the NSWF's equivalence to the Nash Bargaining Solution under specic conditions.

C5. Action-to-neural-drive interface: A new section species the mapping from discrete

RL task assignments to continuous neural drive trajectories C (t), closing the gap between the

allocation layer and the physiological model.

Preserved results. The 3CC-r ODE system, mass conservation, sustainability bound max =

3 Physiological Control: The 3CC-r Model

We model each human worker as a dynamic biological plant governed by mass-action kinetics.
The compartmental structure was introduced by Liu, Brown & Yue [1], extended to submaximal
tasks by Xia & Frey-Law [2], and augmented with a reperfusion multiplier by Looft, Herkert &
Frey-Law [4]. The empirical foundation for muscle endurance modelling traces to the pioneering
endurance-time measurements of Rohmert [26], whose data underpin all subsequent calibration
eorts [6, 3].

4 Constraint Inference: MMICRL

To learn individual worker safety constraints from heterogeneous demonstrations, we use Multi-
Modal Inverse Constrained Reinforcement Learning (MMICRL) [8]. MMICRL builds on Maxi-
mum Entropy IRL [7], Inverse Constrained RL [9], and multi-modal latent variable imitation [10],

to safe policy optimisation by Achiam et al. [11].

was equivalent to maximising mutual information I ( ; z). This is correct only when 1 = 2,
in which case the residual (1 - 2)H [( )] vanishes.

    When 1 = 2, the objective jointly optimises two terms:

   1. 2 I ( ; z): mutual information, promoting between-type separability and within-type coher-

          ence.

      Case analysis:

          variability.

6 Cooperative Task Allocation: Nash Social Welfare

6.1 Terminological Correction

The optimisation in this section is solved by a central planner, not by agents bargaining with each

other. The correct framing is the Nash Social Welfare Function (NSWF), which maximises

the product of agent surpluses. The NSWF is axiomatically connected to Nash's bargaining
theory [17]: Kaneko & Nakamura [21] showed that the NSWF is the unique allocation rule

satisfying Pareto optimality, symmetry, and independence of irrelevant alternatives for N -player

settings, exactly extending Nash's 2-player axioms [17]. The disagreement point formulation
draws on Nash's endogenous threat model [18] and the outside option principle of Binmore,
Shaked & Sutton [20]. The log-transform for gradient-based computation follows Navon et
al. [19].
